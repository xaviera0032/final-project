Car Safety

Welcome


The moment to get your hands on the wheel is within reach. You are excited to be able to drive where ever you please with little to know restrictions. But hold on just one minute. It is understandable that you want to get your driver's license, get an amazingly fast car, and drive off. But there are some things you need to know before you get behind the wheel. Safety is an important thing to learn before hitting the streets. So sit back (or stand its up to you) and enjoy.

Reasoning


Before we talk about major topics, we need to understand why we need safety when behind the wheel. You might think are going to become one of the best drivers who will never get in an accident and knows all the rules. And you might. But realize this. In 2016, there has been a huge amount of accidents. Around 40,000. Most of these are experienced drivers and even they can get hurt. It's better to be safe and to show you and maybe you might learn something you previously might not have known.

What-To-Do


The summer is coming up pretty soon and there is no better time to drive then in the summer. I can give you a set of rules to follow while in a car.

Strap yourself - It is important for you to put your seatbelt on even before you start the engine. It’s not just to avoid getting a ticket but it's the smart thing to do. In case you are in a collision, it helps keep you from hitting your head on the steering wheel or even flying of the window (which has happened countless amount of time).


Slow down - Speed sometimes gives you this adrenaline. You want to test the limits of your car. You want to feel like a badass. Problem is, when you hit a bump or you start to spin out of control, it won’t be fun being in the hospital. Slowing down might feel boring but it helps keep drivers from getting injured.


Don’t drink and drive - This is extremely dangerous not just for you but for others. I am not talking about not drinking water or soda. I’m talking about alcohol. When you drink, your vision become distorted making it so much harder to drive. You might think it’s nothing but thats what alot of people say and it ends up getting their car in an auto repair shop fixing a disaster they made. If you plan on drinking, have a responsible not drunk friend to drive.


Don’t just drive off - Gas is not the only thing that a car needs to survive. Tire pressure, battery, oil, good breaks, and so many other thing needed for the longevity of your car. Make sure you properly check these things out. You don't want to end up in middle of nowhere.
